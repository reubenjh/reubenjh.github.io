Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:

Got blocked on a simple problem.

When trying to turn my design for this site into actual code, I got stuck on what seemed like the simplest problem. We're using a system to for the layout of our sites called a grid framework, where the horizontal space is equally divided into 12 parts. I wanted to make my article content sit more tucked into the middle with more padding on the left and the right sides so there is some whitespace and it doesn't seem like such an intimidaing amount of reading.

I figured it would be easy enough to just divide the horizontal space and leave say 2 parts empty on the left and 2 parts empty on the right, with the article content filling up the central 8 parts. In the left-hand space I put the 'related' side-bar, but I didn't have anything I wanted to put in the right-hand space so I left it empty. When I loaded it up, sure enough there was the left hand space with the 'related' bar, but the content area was taking up the whole remaining space, flowing over into the right-hand 2 spaces I had tried to leave empty. It turns out empty spaces in the framework don't display. It looked ridiculous. The centred lines like headlines weren't centred on the page, they were centred in the 10 spaces it was taking up so slightly skewed to the right.

Not to worry I thought, I'll see what tools the framework has to deal with this. I found a piece of code you can apply to an area to 'offset' it, pushing it right however many of the 12 available horizontal parts that you'd like. But I didn't need to push it right, I needed to push its right boundary left to make the empty space on the right hand side of the page that I wanted. I was stumped with this for about an hour, trying to put empty spaces in the right hand 2 parts of the page so there was technically something there for it to display, or to put some content in there but use code to tell it not to display. All while really not wanting to muddy up the code and make it all really confusing just to create some empty space for a design feature.

Solved a problem in an elegant way.

With the above problem, I decided to try to recreate the tool that the people who had built the grid framework were using to offset areas to the right, but to make a version called 'inset' that did the same in the opposite direction that I actually needed. I trawled through their code until I could figure it out and when I wrote it out and applied it to the main content area for my articles it did exactly what I needed. I can't understand why this isn't a part of the package they supply anyway!
